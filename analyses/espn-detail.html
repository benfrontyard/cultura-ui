Brand Overview

ESPN aims to maintain cultural relevance among Latino Millennials by moving beyond generic coverage toward culturally nuanced sports experiences, bilingual coverage, and authentic Latino voices across digital and broadcast platforms.

Audience Snapshot

Core audience: Bilingual or English-dominant millennials (ages 25–40) in urban U.S. communities, particularly Southwest, California, and Texas.

Cultural identity: Values both heritage and contemporary identity; family fandom and digital-first engagement.

Values

Celebration of sport as cultural legacyCommunity connectionFamily loyaltyAuthenticityRepresentationUpward mobility through shared fandomSports-driven storytelling

Media Habits

Mobile-first and digitally connected; active on Instagram and Facebook; favors live sports on mobile apps and CTV for game day.

Psychographics

Identity-driven, energetic community-builders motivated by authentic representation.

Convenience-minded digital natives who expect bilingual accessibility and shareable highlight moments.

Why It Matters

  1. Latino Millennials are a fast-growing, highly engaged audience shaping U.S. sports fandom.
  2. As digital natives, they set trends in mobile, social, and CTV sports content.
  3. Their purchasing power and family-oriented decision-making drive incremental brand impact.

Strategic Recommendations

  • Pair ESPN.com with Spanish-language sports publishers for incremental reach.
  • Prioritize mobile web and CTV in campaign mix.
  • Use highlight-adjacent placements during key match windows.
